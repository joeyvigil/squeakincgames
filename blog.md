## Designer Diary: 31 Blitz

[card image]

Just for the record I did not design this game. In fact, Its not even that popular on bgg. In fact the prototype cards are made my chatgpt. So what exactly am I contributing? Probably just putting everything together and sharing this game to more people. I was a bit confused as to why the rating on bgg wast that high as I had a great time playing this with some austrians in a hostel a few years ago. I played a lot of card games in bars during that trip. Weird that its a common thing to do in hostel bars. That and pool and just having conversations. I want a small box game, and I think this will do. Especially if the card art, box art, and coins (fingers crossed for having metal coins) are high quality. 

### What did I do?

First thing I needed was a good looking deck. To do this I used ChatGPT. This isn't for production. It just for prototyping. ok. calm down. Here was the prompt I used:

> I want some images of German-suited playing cards for a new card game I'm designing. Help me create a prompt to create them. If possible I want it to generate 36 images for the card faces as well as a card back.

It gave a response, and I asked it to ask me some more specific questions about what I wanted. After that, bada bing bada boom. I had a good prompt. So I go over to a new window and ask it to generate an image. It was honestly really good, besides a few inconsistencies such as the face cards containing no suit symbols, which I had to remedy by adding them on InDesign. I also generated a coin. as well as a card back. It honestly was looking good.

[coin image]

### Rules

I had a working rule sheet and It was good quality, but I still felt like I could improve it. I fed it into ChatGPT (yes, I know AGAIN?). and asked it for advice on what I should edit. I worked with it and picked what I liked and disliked from my current rules and the output. Next I asked ChatGPT to make it more "more compact and easy to understand" which is something I think most rule books need to improve on. Like I was at a Cheyenne gaming convention 2 weeks ago. And we played a game called **Animal kingdom**. In the rules it tell what to do if you tie in an area majority. Each player plays a card, hightest wins, and a 1 beats the 8 (ie hightest). Pretty simple right? Well this one section was explained over an entire page. Like why. Another example Is I purchased **1846: The Race for the Midwest (2005)**. Besides being a complicated game in its own right. The rule book is 24 pages of small text and minimal pictures. Is that really the best you could do for a published game? Maybe people like that, but game designer really should aim to make the game as easy to understand as possible. In conclusion. I think I ended up with an excellent rule sheet.
